Professional Projects

National Water Resources Modeling & Decision Support — Bangladesh

Institute of Water Modelling (IWM), Dhaka, Bangladesh

Contributed to large-scale, government-commissioned water resources and urban infrastructure projects supporting flood risk management, water supply planning, river pollution control, and environmental impact assessment across Bangladesh. These projects informed national and municipal decision-making and provide the applied foundation for my current research on AI-enabled and data-driven infrastructure resilience.

Key focus areas included:

  • Urban stormwater and flood modeling using SWMM, MIKE11/MIKE URBAN, HEC-RAS, and GIS-based analyses
  • Flood inundation mapping, drainage master planning, and feasibility studies for rapidly growing municipalities
  • River water quality modeling and pollution source assessment using MIKE11–ECO Lab and Python-based data analysis
  • Reservoir water balance, hydrodynamic modeling, and operational scenario analysis for industrial water supply systems
  • Environmental and social impact assessment (ESIA/SEIA), including air quality modeling and mitigation planning
  • National-scale hydrologic data quality control, statistical analysis, and database development to support long-term water planning

Representative government clients:

DPHE, DWASA, BEZA, WARPO, and the Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands (Joint Bangladesh–Netherlands Programme).

This professional experience involved close collaboration with government agencies, international partners, and multidisciplinary teams, translating complex modeling outputs into actionable recommendations for infrastructure investment, environmental protection, and long-term resilience planning.
