2D Floodplain Inundation Modeling for a Restored River Reach (HEC-RAS 1D‚Äì2D) Simulation and Analysis of 2D Flooded Area of River Spree (HEC-RAS 1D‚Äì2D Coupled Model)
Technical Report ‚Äî Brandenburg University of Technology, Cottbus | Dec 14, 2018

Authors:
Beza Getnet, Md Abdullah Al Mehedi, Md Zahidul Karim, Monjalina Hoque, Musfiqur Rahman, Sheila Ghimire

Summary:
Diagnosed, stabilized and evaluated a coupled 1D channel + 2D floodplain unsteady-flow model in HEC-RAS to assess inundation patterns along an ~11 km restored reach of the Spree River and test flood mitigation strategies.

What we did:
‚Ä¢ Built a 1D‚Äì2D HEC-RAS unsteady model (1D main channel, 2D floodplain mesh) to simulate flood extent and water surface elevation under multiple scenarios.
‚Ä¢ Performed sensitivity analysis on computational time step and mesh/grid dimensions and compared impacts on predicted flood depths and inundation patterns.
‚Ä¢ Evaluated flood mitigation strategies including dike construction, dredging, and roughness (Manning‚Äôs n) changes to quantify reductions in floodplain inundation.

Methods / Tools:
HEC-RAS (1D‚Äì2D coupling), unsteady flow routing, 2D mesh modeling, scenario analysis, sensitivity analysis, flood-depth comparison across cells.

Key takeaway:
Model results showed sensitivity to time step and roughness and demonstrated that mitigation measures can significantly reduce inundation, while highlighting tradeoffs with ecological goals in renaturation projects.
