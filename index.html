PhD Researcher | AI/ML for Stormwater & Green Stormwater Infrastructure (GSI) Performance | Villanova University

I develop data-driven methods to evaluate and forecast green stormwater infrastructure performance by integrating monitoring data, hydrologic modeling, and machine learning. My work supports risk-informed maintenance and planning to improve urban flood resilience and water quality.

Impact & Relevance

  • Urban resilience: supports proactive screening and maintenance prioritization for stormwater assets to reduce flood and nuisance ponding risk.
  • Decision support: integrates monitoring, modeling (e.g., SWMM), and machine learning to forecast performance and classify risk.
  • Scalable methods: designed for transferability across municipalities using environmental context (land cover/land use) and community vulnerability indicators.

What I Work On

Performance Forecasting

Develop machine-learning models to predict ponding duration and drawdown behavior under varied storm events using observed and simulated datasets.

City-Scale Context & Equity

Quantify how land cover, land use, and social vulnerability relate to GSI siting patterns and performance risk across diverse urban environments.

Risk-Informed Management

Combine predicted performance with condition and sensitivity indicators to support inspection-informed risk alerts and maintenance decision-making.
