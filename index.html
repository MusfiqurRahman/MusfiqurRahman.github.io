AI for Stormwater & Green Infrastructure Resilience

Developing machine learning frameworks to forecast GSI performance and risk, support condition-informed maintenance, and improve sustainable stormwater planning at scale.

About Me

I am a civil and environmental engineering researcher focused on applied AI/ML for stormwater management and green stormwater infrastructure (GSI). I work with monitoring and storm-event data, combine hydrologic and environmental features, and apply machine learning models to generate actionable performance predictions and risk indicators for decision-making.

Tools and methods I frequently use include Python for data processing and machine learning (e.g., XGBoost/LightGBM, support vector machines, and deep learning architectures), process-based hydrologic and hydraulic modeling using EPA SWMM, HEC-HMS, and HEC-RAS, and spatial analysis with ArcGIS and GIS-derived context variables to integrate physical system behavior with data-driven insights.
