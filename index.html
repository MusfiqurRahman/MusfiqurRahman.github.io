PhD Researcher | AI/ML for Stormwater & Green Stormwater Infrastructure (GSI) Performance | Villanova University

I develop data-driven methods to evaluate and forecast green stormwater infrastructure performance by integrating monitoring data, hydrologic modeling, and machine learning. My work supports risk-informed maintenance and planning to improve urban flood resilience and water quality.

Impact & Relevance

  • Urban flood resilience (U.S.-focused, globally informed): Develops data-driven frameworks to support proactive screening, maintenance prioritization, and performance forecasting for stormwater and green stormwater infrastructure (GSI) systems in U.S. cities, informed by applied experience in flood-prone urban environments.
  • Decision-relevant modeling and analytics: Integrates monitoring data, hydrologic and hydraulic modeling (e.g., SWMM, HEC-RAS), and machine learning to generate defensible, actionable insights that support municipal stormwater planning, risk classification, and infrastructure management.
  • Scalable and transferable methods: Designs approaches that generalize across municipalities by incorporating environmental context (land cover and land use) and community vulnerability indicators, drawing on professional experience from large-scale government water and flood management projects in Bangladesh to inform robust, real-world implementation.
